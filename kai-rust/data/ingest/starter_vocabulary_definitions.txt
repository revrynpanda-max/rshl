# ── Common nouns (people, places, things) ───────────────────────────────────
A neuron is a specialized cell that transmits electrical and chemical signals in the nervous system.
A synapse is the junction between two neurons where neural signals are passed from one cell to another.
A brain is the central organ of the nervous system that controls thought, memory, emotion, and behavior.
A memory is information that has been encoded, stored, and can be retrieved from the mind.
A thought is a mental representation or idea that arises in the mind.
A concept is a general idea or abstract notion formed by combining characteristics of particular instances.
A pattern is a regular or recurring arrangement of elements or events.
A signal is a piece of information transmitted from one place or system to another.
A cell is the smallest structural and functional unit of a living organism.
A system is a set of connected parts working together as a whole.

A question is a sentence or phrase that seeks information or an answer.
An answer is a response or reaction to a question or problem.
A conversation is an interactive exchange of thoughts and feelings between two or more people.
A story is a narrative account of events, real or imagined.
A sentence is a grammatical unit that expresses a complete thought.
A word is a single unit of language used to express meaning.
A meaning is the idea or concept that a word, phrase, or symbol represents.
A definition is a statement that explains the meaning of a word or concept.
A context is the circumstances or setting in which something occurs or is interpreted.
A reason is a cause, explanation, or justification for an event, action, or belief.

Reasoning is the process of drawing conclusions based on evidence or premises.
Logic is the systematic study of valid inference and correct reasoning.
Intuition is the ability to understand something immediately without conscious reasoning.
Imagination is the capacity to form mental images of things not present to the senses.
Truth is the quality of being in accord with fact or reality.
Meaning is the significance, purpose, or importance of something.
Identity is the characteristics and qualities that make a person or thing distinct.
Purpose is the reason for which something is done or exists.
Ethics is the branch of knowledge dealing with moral principles governing behavior.
Morality is the principles concerning the distinction between right and wrong behavior.
